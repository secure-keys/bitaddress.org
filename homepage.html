How To Use Bitaddress To Generate Bitcoin Wallet Addresses From An Existing Private Key Or Seed Phrase

On the Generator app, select "Wallet Details" from the tab, and paste your details directly in the input field. We recommend you avoid typing directly in the box; instead, copy and paste your information. This should be your standard practice henceforth whenever you need to enter your private key or seed phrase anywhere while connected to the internet.

When you submit a Bitcoin wallet private key, the following are generated:

• Standard Bitcoin address & corresponding QR code,
• Compressed format & corresponding QR code,
• Public keys (standard 130 characters [0-9A-F] & compressed 66 characters [0-9A-F]).

If you submit a mnemonic seed/master seed/recovery phrase, in an attempt to create a new bitcoin wallet or create backup keys for your existing wallet, the corresponding private keys will be generated for you in the following formats:

• Private Key WIF (51 characters base58, starts with a '5' WIF format),
• Private Key WIF Compressed (52 characters base58, starts with a 'K' or 'L'),
• Private Key Hexadecimal Format (64 characters [0-9A-F]),
• Private Key Base64 (44 characters).

For other usages (including single wallet generation, bulk wallets generation, brain wallet generation, et cetera), please select the appropriate label from the menu tab in the Bitcoin wallet address generator app>
